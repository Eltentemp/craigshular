Just Getting Started

I am a junior at Poway to Palomar Middle College, which is a program where high school students take real college-level courses at Palomar College. I chose this program because I wanted more of a challenge and knew I would get bored in a regular high school environment. It pushes me to stay organized and take responsibility for my own schedule in a way most students my age do not have to.

I have always been interested in aviation and how things work. I like the technical side of it, the engineering, the problem solving, and the way every small part has to function perfectly for the whole system to work. That kind of detail-focused thinking carries over into everything else I do too.

Through my CTAE internship I got to work in real professional environments with real deadlines. I helped coordinate a graduation at Westview High School where I managed the flow of the event, helped guests find their seats, and worked with staff to keep things running on time. I also worked on the PUSD Scholarship Master List where I had to keep data accurate and up to date under a deadline. Neither of those were practice assignments. They were real tasks that actually mattered and I took them seriously.

Outside of school and work I stay pretty active. Rock climbing is probably my biggest hobby right now. I mostly do outdoor bouldering which means no ropes, just you and the rock. It takes a lot of focus and patience and I have gotten a lot better at staying calm when something is hard and not giving up. I think that mindset carries over into other things in my life too.

I also go to the gym pretty regularly. I started going to get stronger for climbing but now I just like having that part of my routine. It keeps me focused and gives me more energy throughout the day. Going consistently has taught me a lot about discipline and showing up even when you do not feel like it.

Mountain biking is something I have been getting into lately. I like riding trails outdoors and figuring out lines through technical sections. It is a good mix of physical and mental because you have to read the terrain ahead of you while also reacting in the moment. It is a lot of fun and I am still learning.

When I am not doing any of that I spend a lot of time with my friends. We are usually outdoors doing something or just hanging out. Having people around you who push you to do more and try new things makes a big difference and I am lucky to have that group.

I also volunteer at the Frosted Faces Foundation, a rescue organization for senior dogs. The work is hands-on and not always glamorous, but it has taught me that real reliability means showing up every time, not just when it feels exciting.
