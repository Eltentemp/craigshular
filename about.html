I'm a junior at Poway to Palomar Middle College (PPMC) — a program that puts high school students in real college-level courses before they ever set foot on a traditional campus. That's not an accident for me. It's the kind of challenge I look for, because I've learned that the best way to grow is to operate in environments most people aren't ready for yet.

I have a deep interest in aviation and engineering — the science of how things work, how systems fit together, and what it takes to operate in high-stakes environments. That fascination drives the way I approach everything I do: with precision, calm under pressure, and a mindset that always asks what's next.

Through the CTAE (Career Technical and Applied Education) program, I've had the opportunity to move beyond textbooks and into real professional environments. Coordinating events. Managing data. Communicating across diverse teams of adults. These aren't simulations — they're the actual work, and I do it well.

Outside of school and work, I also have experience in coding — specifically game development and AI-assisted development. Building things from scratch teaches you how to think systematically, debug problems under pressure, and follow through when the solution isn't obvious. Those are engineering skills, no matter what domain they're applied in.

I also volunteer at the Frosted Faces Foundation, working with senior rescue dogs. It's a different kind of work — quiet, unglamorous, and requiring real patience — but it taught me something I carry everywhere: show up the same way whether anyone is watching or not. That consistency is character. It's the thing that separates people who can handle pressure from people who only perform when it matters.

I have my own car and cover hours and situations that most students my age can't. I'm not here to be average. I'm building a track record — and this is just the beginning.
